What is D.O?

D.O is shorthand for "Diskrimineringsombudsmannen" which is an administrative authority in Sweden. It handles legal cases if someone has been discriminated by the society. In Sweden, it is illegal to discriminate and treat people with disabilities different from other people. Therefore, D.O is there to pursue legal cases on the behalf of those disabled who might have been discriminated.

Important to note, is that D.O does not pursue legal cases between private individuals. They only handle cases where corporations, institutions and such are involved.

How to get help from D.O

In order to pursue a legal case with help from D.O, you have to write to D.O and describe in detail what has happened. Describe when it happened, where it happened and who was involved. D.O will then assess your information and decide whether or not they will pursue a legal case based on your information.
